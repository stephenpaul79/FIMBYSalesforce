Part 1 · Signing in

You sign in once. The bridge takes over.

Your phone never sees your Salesforce password. It gets a one-time claim ticket, hands it to the Auth Bridge, and the Bridge does the real trade — server-side.

Think of the Bridge as a train conductor. On day one you buy a 30-day pass: you punch your PIN into the station machine yourself, so the machine keeps your PIN and the conductor never sees it. In return your phone gets a pass that works only on this transit line, never your bank card. Upstash is the transit system's database, registering your pass so it is honored for a month without you buying a new one. Every day after, you just show the pass and the conductor unlocks the train door for that trip.

Part 2 · Every time you open the app

A fresh one-time pass, then the neighbourhood loads.

The Bridge mints a short-lived signed URL into Salesforce. The app opens it in a WebView. From there, everything you see — feed, posts, library, messages — is served live from Experience Cloud. Push notifications ride the same path in reverse.

The upside of this design: because the real UI lives on Salesforce, I can fix a bug or add a feature and neighbours see it within minutes — no App Store review, no waiting for people to update the app.

Why this design

Four wins from those four boxes.

Zero-trust on the device

Salesforce credentials never leave the server. Lost phone? The wristband it holds expires in minutes and can be revoked in seconds.

Ship fixes the same day

Because the real UI lives on Salesforce, I deploy, publish, and neighbours see it instantly — no App Store review.

Stolen tokens self-destruct

Refresh tokens rotate. If an old one is replayed, the whole family gets revoked automatically.

One codebase, two stores

The Expo shell is identical on iOS and Android. Salesforce does the heavy lifting — features land on both platforms at once.

Tap → sign in → wristband → frontdoor → neighbourhood. Everything else is just Salesforce doing Salesforce things — wrapped in something neighbours actually want to open.
