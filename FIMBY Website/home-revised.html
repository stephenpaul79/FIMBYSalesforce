We're more connected than ever.

So why do so many of us still feel alone?

Not another marketplace.
Not another feed.

The big platforms are built to keep you scrolling, not to help you know the people next door. Here, neighbours share what they already have: a borrowed tool, a shared Costco run, a spare hour. A block leans on itself a little more, and needs to buy a little less.

Designed to stay local

FIMBY keeps things close to home. You'll see the same neighbours, not thousands of strangers, so names become familiar and small acts of care build real relationships.

And unlike apps built to buy, sell, or give away, FIMBY is built to foster relationships. You loan a neighbour your drill, it comes back, and so does the conversation: a face you'll see again, not a stranger across town.

What if where you live felt a little more like family?

Not sentimental. Just people who know your name, notice when you're carrying too much, and leave room at the table.

Here's what that looks like

Small, everyday ways to ask, offer, and stay connected with people close enough to actually show up.

Ask & Offer

Need a hand moving a couch? Got extra tomato starts? Ask, or offer. Both are normal here.

Lend & Borrow

Tools, kitchen gear, books, from people on your block.

Help Nearby

Borrow a skill like you'd borrow a drill: a neighbour who fixes bikes, tutors, translates, or lends a hand.

Gather

Backyard dinners, block parties, and standing rhythms like a weekly movie night or monthly soup night you can count on.

Shared Life

Thank-yous, stories, prayer if that's your thing. Never required.

Local orgs

Churches, centres, and community groups post meals, rides, and check-ins: steady tables neighbours can count on, right in the feed.

More than an app

The feed is not the point.

FIMBY isn't really about software. It's about refusing something we've quietly accepted: that people can live a few doors apart for years and still remain strangers.

A borrowed pot becomes a conversation. A shared meal becomes a check-in. A thank-you becomes a story the neighbourhood remembers. The one who lent the ladder in June is the one asking for a ride in November. Nobody stays the helper. Nobody stays the helped.

There's an old name for this: mutual aid. Not charity flowing one direction, just neighbours trading places, season after season.

We know this works, because it already has. Not a bigger network, but a neighbourhood that feels a little more like family.

Not a thought experiment

Twenty years of an ancient practice

Neighbouring is not our invention. People have been doing it for as long as there have been neighbours: on this land for thousands of years, and on these blocks long before us. FIMBY is just our attempt to keep an old practice alive in a new time.

Our part of the story is twenty-plus years in Vancouver's Downtown Eastside, on blocks that carry more than their share: block parties, shared meals, borrowed ladders, and showing up when someone was carrying too much. For us that practice is rooted in Christian tradition, and it stays open to everyone. The app is newer than that. What stayed the same is the radius: a block, a building, a few streets, where you see the same faces again and small acts of care can turn into real trust.

It's not a crisis line, or a substitute for shelters, detox, or health care. It's the everyday kind of neighbouring that sits alongside those supports, never in their place.

This isn't theory. During our initial pilot, one neighbour asked for book recommendations and ended up inside another's home for the first time, after living a block apart for nine years.

Made for real life

No one gets left out

You don't have to be online to belong here. Someone you trust, a neighbour, a family member, or a church volunteer, can take part on your behalf: posting, responding, and messaging as you. Built for elders, people with low tech confidence, or anyone who'd rather have someone close help them join in.

Parents can do the same for a kid in their household: a parent-managed family profile, so a child can be known by name in the neighbourhood while a parent is always the one at the keyboard.

And you decide how neighbours can show up for you. Help is welcomed, never assumed.

A few practical things, before you go

Will this just be another dead app?

FIMBY is neighbourhood-sized on purpose: the same faces, again and again, not a city-wide feed chasing engagement.

Who can see my information?

Only your neighbourhood, never the whole city. Your contact details are never shared automatically; you choose what to share, with whom, and can take it back anytime.

Isn't lending my stuff risky?

A little. Trusting people always is. But it isn't blind. Before anyone can borrow, a neighbour who knows them vouches: "I know this person, they're a good egg." So you're not gambling on a stranger, you're trusting a neighbour's judgement and passing that same chance along. You risk trusting them, they trust you back. That's how neighbours slowly become something more like family.

Is it free?

Yes, free to you. No ads, no premium tiers, no data sales. FIMBY is a non-profit endeavour built to serve the people who use it, sustained by donations and community contributions.
