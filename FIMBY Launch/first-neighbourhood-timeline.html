The one thing this timeline is for

FIMBY doesn't need a big network. It needs one small network that feels alive — roughly 15–30 neighbours in V6A who keep crossing paths, closing loops, and slowly trusting each other. Everything below serves that single outcome.

The app is live but the neighbourhood isn't yet. Your job for six months is to be the network's heartbeat until it has its own.

Three principles to carry through every month:

  • Offline first. The relationship precedes the app. Every recruitment, welcome, and recovery move works better in person.
  • Protect the first ask. An ask that gets no response is a trust wound. In these six months, no ask goes unanswered — even if you have to hand-route it yourself.
  • Cadence beats volume. One predictable weekly post makes a small network feel alive. Ten sporadic ones don't.
Month −2 · July

Seed the core

The app is essentially empty right now — that's fine, nobody's watching yet. This month is entirely offline: assemble the 10–20 people and the content engine that will make the feed worth opening in September.

Watch for: anyone on your list who wants in but struggles with the phone. That's not a lost member — that's your first supported participant. Pair them with someone they trust who can act on their behalf.
Month −1 · August

Make it feel alive before the doors open

A newcomer in September should walk into a room where something is already happening. This month the core group builds habits, and you rehearse every loop end-to-end at least once.

Guardrail: if the core group hasn't formed by late August — fewer than 10 active, library thin, no gathering held — move the launch date. A quiet extra month costs nothing. A dead launch month feeds the "another dead app" story and is expensive to undo.
Launch · September

Open the doors, close every loop

Signups open to all of V6A. Your job flips from builder to concierge: every newcomer gets a human welcome, and no loop is allowed to stall. This is the highest-touch month of the six.

Watch for the wobble: a dip in weeks two and three post-launch is normal, not failure. Respond with a gathering, not a notification blast — FIMBY doesn't tap shoulders, you do.
Month +1 · October

From concierge to habit

Launch energy fades in October — habits have to replace it. Growth now travels through members rather than posters, and pieces of your job start moving to other hands.

Month +2 · November

Let the network carry weight

Time to find out what the network can hold on its own. You deliberately step back in places, watch what stalls, and step in only where loops actually break.

Months +3 & +4 · December

Prove it's alive, write it down

The closing test: can this neighbourhood run without you for two weeks? And can what you learned become a kit someone else could follow? (The companion playbook file is where those lessons land.)

Guardrail: expansion is a supply decision, not a demand decision. A second neighbourhood launched without its own anchor host, orgs, and core group produces a dead hub — and a dead hub is worse than a waitlist.

Your recurring rhythm

The months change; this loop doesn't. It's the whole job in miniature, from September onward.

Most days

  • Sweep for unanswered asks — hand-route anything stalling
  • Welcome new signups within 24 hours

Every week

  • Confirm the org post went out
  • Attend or enable the standing gathering
  • Post or prompt one thank-you naming real neighbours
  • Check in on one quiet member, in person if you can
  • Write three lines in the operator's log

Every month

  • Dashboard review (below)
  • Reciprocity pass: only-givers and only-receivers
  • Helper check-in — are their roles still the right size?

What "alive" means here

FIMBY's goal is relationship outside the app, so health is measured in loops closed and reciprocity — never in attention.

MeasureHealthy signal by December
Ask fulfillmentEvery ask answered — 100% while you're concierge; at least 90% within 48 hours once you step back.
Time to first responseUnder 24 hours, consistently.
Loops closed per monthLoans completed and returned, bulk buys fulfilled, gatherings held — trending up, month over month.
Reciprocity ratioAt least half of active members have both given and received.
Vouch coverageEvery member vouched; no one stuck at the borrowing gate.
Repeat pairsThe same two neighbours interacting a second and third time — growing steadily.
Time to first actionNew members complete one real action within their first week.

Deliberately not measured: daily active users, session length, post counts, scroll depth. If those ever become the scoreboard, the neighbourhood has already lost the plot.

Any month

Red flags & how to recover

  • An ask sits unanswered past 24 hours. Hand-route it today. This is the single most damaging cold-start event — the asker's worst fear, confirmed.
  • The feed goes silent for three days or more. Don't fill it with noise. Check the org rhythm first, then announce or hold a gathering — one real event repopulates a week of feed.
  • Signups pile up without activation or first actions. Your bottleneck is onboarding, not demand. Slow the promotion, speed up the welcomes.
  • You're the only one posting. Stop posting and start prompting. Ask a member to share the thing you were about to share.
  • Helpers going quiet. Their role is probably the wrong size. Shrink it before they disappear — a smaller kept commitment beats a bigger abandoned one.
  • A dead fortnight. Recoverable — one warm gathering resets it. A dead quarter is what reads as "another dead app". Act at the fortnight.
