One thing a week

The lighter, do-it version of the launch plan — shaped so you can just execute, sized to one hour a day and one real conversation a week, and leaning on Strathcona Vineyard rather than on you alone.

Start here

You already have what most launches spend months chasing: twenty years in V6A, a church with staff behind you, and a couple of close friends who'd help. So this isn't a recruiting campaign. It's a handful of warm conversations with people you already know.

Your project this week is not "launch FIMBY." It's the one small action at the top of the list below — nothing past it.

Two ground rules that make the rest work:

  • One shaped action a week. Every step below is already given its shape — no figuring-out-what-it-means before you can do it. If a step ever feels vague, it's not ready; cut it smaller.
  • You carry the least. The church carries the feed's rhythm; a friend holds one role; you mostly show up warmly for people you know.
However the week finds you

Green weeks and couch weeks

Some weeks you'll kick butt; some weeks you won't leave the couch. The plan is built for both — every week below has a full version and a couch version, and either one counts as done.

Green week

You've got energy. Do the week's action as written — the in-person, warm version.

Couch week

You're low. Do the smaller fallback under each week (usually a text instead of a meeting, or a solo task). Still done. Nothing is live yet, so nobody is waiting — resting costs nothing.

The same grace the app gives. FIMBY believes in quiet hours, seasons, and never punishing people for being human. You get that too. A couch week isn't falling behind — it's the app's own values, pointed back at its founder.

The next eight weeks

One action each, in order. Do this week's and stop.

1
This week

Coffee with the one friend who's easiest to be around.

Shape it: One invite, a real date and time, about 45 minutes, coffee near you. No agenda past "let me show you this thing I'm building." Show them FIMBY on your phone and think out loud about V6A.

🌙Couch version: Just send the invite for a date next week. Booking it counts as done.
2
Next

Ask that friend to hold one small, named role.

Shape it: One concrete job with edges — "keep half an eye out for posts nobody's answered, and help welcome a new neighbour now and then." Not "help me with FIMBY." Your first co-steward, so you're never the only one holding it.

🌙Couch version: Send the ask as a voice note or text instead of in person.
3
Then

Sit with the Vineyard staff person closest to you.

Shape it: One conversation. Ask for one recurring post — a meal, a drop-in, a ride offer — and who on staff will own putting it up each week. This is the church carrying the feed's pulse, which is the job you most want off your own plate.

🌙Couch version: Email the one-paragraph version instead of meeting.
4
A solo week — good for a low one

Write down eight people you already know.

Shape it: Eight names — Vineyard or V6A people who'd get what FIMBY is. Circle the three easiest to approach. Just names on paper; no contact yet. This is the "find founding neighbours" task, already given its shape. No social battery required.

🌙Couch version: Write three names instead of eight. Still done.
5
Then

Show FIMBY to one of your circled three.

Shape it: One person, one bounded coffee or porch visit with a stated start and finish. Same warm show-and-tell as week 1 — you're just widening the circle by one.

🌙Couch version: Text them to float it — "want to see a thing I'm building? No rush." Scheduling it counts.
6
Then

List a few lendable things — with your co-steward.

Shape it: One working hour together (this is the week's social touch). You each list three things you'd lend, in the app. Six real items beats an empty library — and it's a gentle, side-by-side kind of hangout.

🌙Couch version: Do it solo from the couch — list your own three items, skip the meetup.
7
Then

Confirm the church's first post is going up.

Shape it: One short check-in with the staff owner from week 3 — confirm the recurring post has a day and is actually being posted. Confirm it; don't redesign it. This is your first real feed heartbeat.

🌙Couch version: A confirming text is enough.
8
The small payoff

Hold a tiny pilot gathering — co-hosted, not solo.

Shape it: Small, at the church or a friend's place, co-hosted with your co-steward. Invite whoever from your list said yes. Bounded — a stated start and finish (60–75 min), food, one clear purpose: "come try FIMBY together." You are not hosting alone. This is where more helpers naturally surface.

🌙Couch version: Shrink it — three people at a coffee shop — or slide it a week. Reduce, don't cancel.

When a week goes sideways

Three rules for the hard ones

  • Never miss twice. Skip a week's action and the only rule is: don't skip the next one too. Reduce it, don't rewrite the plan. One missed text is not a verdict on FIMBY.
  • If a step demands the tea-and-cookies you, shrink it. When something feels like it needs your warmest, most available self, that's the signal to cut it to the smallest honest version — or move it to a green week. A wave is not a broken promise.
  • Bounded by default. Every invite names a start, a finish, and a purpose. Edges are what make it possible to say yes — to your neighbours, and to yourself.

Your whole scoreboard

Not signups, not activity, not anyone else's yardstick. One question at the end of each week:

"Did I take one honest, bounded action this week?"

Yes — couch week or green week — is a good FIMBY week. The neighbourhood's health metrics (loops closed, reciprocity) live in the original timeline for when you're ready for them. For now, this is the only number that's yours.
