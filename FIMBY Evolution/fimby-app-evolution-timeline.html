How to read this: each chapter groups the planning work into a phase of the build. Dates are the creation dates of the underlying plans, so they show when the thinking happened, not when every line shipped. The app was submitted to both stores in mid-June and approved in the last week of June 2026 (Chapter 10).

1
Feb 14 – 17, 2026

Foundations & the warm aesthetic

Before features, a feel: a feed that looks handmade, not Silicon Valley.

  • Aligned every post composer to one shared pattern
  • Renamed the "marketplace" into neighbourly Asks & Offers
  • Built out detail screens and the first My Stuff hub
  • The earthy colour overhaul — warm parchment, no harsh whites
  • Stood up the --fimby-* CSS token framework (light & dark from day one)
  • First pass at Contacts & private messaging and a card image grid
2
Feb 18 – 22, 2026

Identity, accessibility & one inbox

Make it usable for everyone, and put all the talking in one place.

  • Profile & Settings pages; reworked account deactivation
  • Neighbourhood search; an LWC token + accessibility sweep
  • Earth-tone feed colours and full-width row cards
  • A unified communications hub for messages
  • The onboarding modal and a clearer step flow
  • Response-thread redesign; the celebration component is born
  • Notifications overhaul + a two-column desktop layout
3
Feb 22 – 25, 2026

The Scale-Back

A deliberate turn away from engagement mechanics. A doorbell, not a slot machine.

  • Killed timer polling; swapped count badges for quiet dot indicators
  • Notification batching, a 90-day story TTL, no more broadcast push
  • Auto-cleanup of old conversations
  • Redesigned messaging from chat-style to calmer email-style
  • Gmail-style inbox; mobile date formatting
  • Renamed Stories to "Shared Life"; standardized widths & breakpoints
4
Feb 28 – Mar 7, 2026

Apex hardening, Bulk Buys & humane push

Strengthen the engine, then add the features that pool a street's resources.

  • An Apex best-practices audit across the codebase
  • The Bulk Buy feature — split a Costco run with your neighbours (many iterations)
  • Reservation-aware detail & feed
  • Push integration → the humane push system (quiet hours, the sleeping panda)
  • Governor-limit optimization for scale
  • The neighbourhood growth framework — scale like yeast, not a network
5
Mar 9 – 13, 2026

A QA wave & the acting-as identity framework

Test everything — and let a trusted neighbour act on someone's behalf.

  • Home-feed state preservation; homepage QA and discrepancy hunt
  • A battery of QA runbooks (asks/offers, lending, stories, messages, my stuff, notifications, profile)
  • Composer split + multi-image posts
  • Community Group integration
  • The "Logged in as" framework — support people acting for elders & neighbours
  • Inactive-post CTA guards; character counters
6
Mar 14 – 22, 2026

Events & persona-first detail pages

Three kinds of gathering — and detail pages built around the person, not the record.

  • Frontdoor quiet-hours sync; kebab identity-menu redesign
  • Simplified "posting as" down to one clear line
  • Persona-first detail pages; desktop header actions; DOM confetti
  • The Event types analysis → Gathering / Open Event / Community Event
  • Event icons & info; event gap and notification remediation; reminders
  • Feed CTA modal system; universal button styling
7
Mar 27 – Apr 14, 2026

Lending, email digests & the moderator dashboard

Round out the borrowing lifecycle, the inbox-that-comes-to-you, and the tools to keep it safe.

  • Org-contact messaging parity; segmented thermometer; an economization plan
  • Library lending state-awareness, item-return modal, notification audit
  • Lending-request UX overhaul; messages-list overhaul; unified swipe gestures
  • The My Stuff redesign (many passes); library category icons & colours
  • The email alert system → rich digest cards; "Say thanks" on detail pages
  • The neighbourhood moderator dashboard + triage-first revamp
8
Apr 20 – 30, 2026

Security, legal & launch prep

Make the app match its promises before the stores ever see it.

  • Security audits (web + mobile); a universal back button
  • Salesforce deletion flow + deletion-parity audit
  • Terms-of-service acceptance; a Playwright e2e suite
  • Content-moderation MVP; UGC publish-readiness
  • Prelaunch product alignment — app behaviour to match the legal/help pages
  • The operations playbook
9
May 17 – 30, 2026

Trust, vouching & launch readiness

Decide who belongs, and how — then get the app ready to face store review.

  • The trust-vetting / vouching system (vouching is personal)
  • Onboarding & walkthrough overhaul; LWC conditional-directives migration
  • Pause-vs-delete account; universal links & URL masking
  • Acting-as banner gap analysis; replaced Chatter with native Story Comments
  • Notification usability audit + coverage matrix; digest tweaks
  • A launch-grade QA sweep; native signup form; warm rebrand of the pre-auth screen
  • Push-subscribe fix; auth-bridge & mobile security hardening
10
June 2026

The final mile — submission & approval

One last sweep of hardening and richness, then out the door for review.

  • App-install prompt; mobile deep-link & OAuth UX; sharing-gap remediation
  • Inbox badge sync; avatar crop; post-edit parity; home load timing; foreground resume
  • The resilience roadmap + phases (Help Nearby / skills, pantry, repost recurrence, profile activity)
  • Minor-profiles strategy; smooth page transitions; multi-device push
  • Help & FAQ refresh; durable blocking; quiet smart-push; split notifications
  • Safety features; session-timeout recovery; redacted profiles; the live guided tour
  • App Store & Google Play submission, review, and approval
